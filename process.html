How it works

GridForge turns messy outreach into a clean, repeatable workflow. You provide the right inputs, it generates a tight draft, and you review + send with confidence. The goal: less fluff, more replies.

1) Provide inputs

Business, recipient, objective, context, and constraints. Clarity over volume.

2) Generate a draft

Professional copy tuned to intent, industry, and the recipient’s role.

3) Review & ship

Edit quickly, keep what works, iterate what doesn’t. Then send.

What you should have ready before generating:

Recipient clarity

Name, company, role, and what they likely care about (pain, priority, KPI).

Your offer

What you do in one sentence. If you can’t say it simply, fix that first.

A single objective

Call, intro, meeting, referral, or reply. Don’t cram three asks into one message.

Single Draft workflow

Use this when you want one high-quality message you can send immediately. GridForge keeps the structure tight: context → relevance → ask → easy close.

Step A — Set the scene

Add business + recipient basics. The system uses this to avoid generic “AI soup” and anchor the message in real context.

Step B — Define intent

Choose message type (Email / LinkedIn DM / Follow-up / Inbound reply). Each type has different length, tone, and pacing rules.

Step C — Add constraints

Constraints make drafts better. Examples: “keep under 90 words”, “no buzzwords”, “ask for 15 minutes”, “mention their recent expansion”.

What the draft includes

A clean opening, one relevance hook, a direct ask, and a low-friction close. No long paragraphs. No “synergy” garbage.

Why review matters

AI drafts are strong starting points, not holy scripture. You should always sanity-check facts, names, and tone before sending.

How to iterate fast

Change one thing at a time: opening line, relevance hook, or the ask. Keep what gets replies and reuse it.

Bulk Generator workflow

Use Bulk when you want one message per row in a CSV. You provide structured data, GridForge produces structured drafts — and you export results for outreach.

Step 1 — Download the template

Grab the Excel template so your headers match what GridForge expects. If headers don’t match, you’ll get missing fields or ugly outputs.

Step 2 — Fill rows cleanly

Each row = one recipient. Keep notes short and factual. Example notes: “hiring SDRs”, “opened Chicago office”, “uses HubSpot”.

Step 3 — Upload, preview, generate

Upload the CSV, preview the parsed rows, then generate all drafts. After generation, download results as a CSV for sending.

Headers expected

name, company, title, email, notes, industry, goals, messageType

Best practices for Bulk

Don’t put essays in notes. Add one relevance hook per person. The quality of your inputs controls the quality of your outputs.

Results you can export

You’ll typically export name/company/email + the generated message. Then use your own outbound system to send responsibly.

GridForge is built around one principle: strong inputs → clean drafts → fast iteration. If you want “magic,” go to a horoscope. If you want repeatable outcomes, this is it.
